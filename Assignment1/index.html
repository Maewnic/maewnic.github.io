1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The website greeted with a drawing animation, classical music, and zoomed out into a whole sketch. The sketch looks messy, but in the silhouette of an architecture.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Moving my mouse around the sketch parallax
  • Moving my mouse inside the word ‘Sculpting Harmony’, and found a trail of words
  • Scrolling up and down to forward and reverse the video in the background and animation.
  • Pause and play the video
  • Turn the sound off and on
  • Click into the menu.
  • Click read more
  • Click on the video

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spend the most time on the Introduction page. This could be because I was introduced to the website and discovered many different interactions. From the parallax, the mouse trails on the word ‘Sculpting Harmony’, and the video-like scrolling with stretched words.

4) What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

My most common action is just scrolling up and down, watching the animation flow with the scroll movement.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The design of this website is clean and easy to read, while also being artistic and incorporating subtle interactions. The sketches, along with classical music, make this website feel fancy and uplifting. I think the website communicates well how it is design-driven, reflecting the content that explores the design and construction of the Walt Disney Concert Hall.

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

Many of the interactions very well enhance the aesthetic of the website. Although some is a little gimmicky, it shows how artistic the website is.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The animations on the website felt repetitive after scrolling for a while, so it’s not the animations that kept the viewer staying on the website for long. It seems to be mostly for reading. If the viewer is interested in reading, they could stay for quite some time, perhaps up to an hour.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

Due to it being a single-page web. It is intended to be more for reading in a linear, following a timeline in order.

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

This website seems to be referencing something more physical, like an exhibition. The viewer has a starting point. Along the journey, there are pictures and videos with descriptions labelled, being displayed from left to right.

10) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

A website doesn’t need to be limited to a blog-like format. It could be referencing a physical space, but with the flexibility, control, and interactions of a website.

11) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

I feel inspired to try something new when creating a website.

12) What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

Sometimes the animation is a little dragging. The text constantly switches between far left and far right, making it annoying to change the point of focus.

13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

I am most satisfied with the aesthetic and the animation, particularly when elements move in and out, overlap, and videos seamlessly transition.
